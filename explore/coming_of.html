Our philosophy

Coming of age ceremonies are ancient practices, but as a society we seem to have lost the recipe and as a result many of our children enter into adolescence or young adulthood unprepared.
Our coming of age custom ceremonies and programs prepare children and their families for the next phase in life with deeper intention, better tools, and stronger connections with themselves, each other, their tribes, and nature. This is also great for adults that didn't experience it growing up themselves.

A conscious and intentional way of stepping into adolescence and adulthood

Reflect and release

Recognizing and appreciating what's being left behind as a child or adolescent is important for letting go of old roles and patterns and move on to what's next.


Explore and prepare

Accepting uncertainty, exploring new possibilities, learning new skills, and creating healthy new relationships are all part of any good coming of age experience.


Embrace and integrate

Defining and embracing a unique path forward for the individual and their tribe leads to celebration and the person assuming a new role in their community.


We'll help you create a path forward and guide you every step of the way.

Every person and journey is unique, so our initiates co-create their own new and empowering story in which they discover themselves and take responsibility for the decisions that set the course of their future.

You focus on learning & growing, we'll handle the details.

Like wedding planners, we'll take care of the logistics and details so the initiate and their family can focus their time and energy on digging deep to get the most of this challenging and exciting event.

Your questions answered
Is there an ideal age for a coming of age ceremony?
Based on common child developmental phases coming of age ceremonies are typically most effective as children become adolescents between ages 11 and 14 and also as they become young adults between 17 and 20. Coming of age experiences are valuable at any age for people that did not experience them earlier.
What's the process and timeline for planning a personalized Coming of Age ceremony?
Here's a summary of the process:
1) We start with a no-charge consultation over the phone to discuss your situation and desires and make sure we're a good fit for you and your family
2) After receiving your deposit, we meet in person or over the phone to get to know each other better and begin the process of planning the experience
3) We send you a draft of the plan and timelines by email and you reply with feedback and change requests
4) Once you're satisfied with the plan and timelines we book the date for the session and begin making preparations according to the plan
5) We meet, learn, grow, and connect according to the plan and repeat this until all steps are completed and the ending celebration is done
What makes your celebrants uniquely qualified to work with my family?
Cal and Mara are well-balanced, compassionate, family-oriented celebrants that are educated, experienced, and open minded. They understand child development very well, have researched life transitions extensively, have experienced them hand as people and as parents, and are certified and ordained through multiple organizations.
Do you provide this service nation wide?
Yes, our celebrants are ready and willing to support families wherever they may be. Services provided outside of our SE Michigan/NW Ohio home region will require that clients cover reasonable travel costs.
Are these services “spiritually” or "religiously" oriented?
They can be, or they can not be, depending on the client. We serve families of all spiritual perspectives or religious faiths and will create and deliver services that honor virtually any respectful and reasonable belief system.
