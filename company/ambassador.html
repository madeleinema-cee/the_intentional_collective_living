Who you are



You have experience in - and a deep passion for - one of the big life challenges we support.

You want to give something back by helping others that are dealing with what you’ve experienced.

You want to be part of a team of passionate, kind and creative volunteers just like yourself.



What you’ll be doing

Listen

Listen to people and organizations to understand what they need and how we can best help them

Connect

Connect people that need help and resources with organizations and people that can provide them

Lead

Assume team leadership roles as desired and available to continue personal growth and development

Give hope and inspiration

Use your own knowledge and experiences to give hope and inspiration to others in need

Coordinate

Work as part of the volunteer ambassador team to coordinate efforts for best impact and results

Represent

Represent us in the community and advocate back to us on behalf of “your people"

What you can get from us



Satisfaction from serving

Putting your own knowledge and experience to use helping others facing similar challenges can be hugely satisfying and can also be an important part of your own healing experience.







Learning and growth opportunities

As part of the volunteer ambassador team you can learn from others on the team and benefit from the training we provide. If chosen to be a "lead" ambassador you will coordinate the efforts of others in your area and develop valuable leadership skills.







Knowledge, connections and resources

Build meaningful connections with our leadership team, other ambassadors, and others in the community you serve. To maximize the impact of your passion and efforts, we also offer our ambassadors a rich collection of resources and tools.

4) Be responsive so people know you care and be collaborative to be an effective team player

Do ambassadors get paid?
Ambassadors don't receive monetary pay for their efforts. The benefits they get are the satisfaction of doing something good for the sake of good; a deeper and broader set of connections to others in their community; more knowledge, better skills, and practical experience for helping others; something that looks good on a resume; and a sense of belonging for being part of a team dedicated to changing the world for the better.


How much time will being an ambassador take?
That will vary quite a bit depending on how involved the ambassador chooses to be. At a minimum ambassadors will spend 2 to 3 hours a quarter getting training from us to learn about the role and to learn about tools and resources at your disposal, and then another 1 to 2 hours a quarter in collaboration calls with other ambassadors in your area of interest. From there it will depend on how many people reach out to an ambassador for help, how active he/she is on community forums, and what events the ambassador chooses to be part of.


How do I apply to become an ambassador?
Please complete the online application form to let us know you're interested. We'll acknowledge your application right away, and then one of our team will follow up with you within 5 working days to ask questions or schedule an interview.


How do I apply to be the "lead" ambassador in my area?
There will be one lead ambassador for each major life challenge we support, and since we're early in the process of building our ambassador team we're looking for some strong leaders to help us build and manage small teams. Lead ambassador candidates should meet the basic ambassador criteria above and also:
1) Be excellent communicators and team players;
2) Have demonstrated leadership experience;
3) Be generally known and respected in their field of interest;
4) Be willing to commit time and energy to not only help others but also coordinate the efforts of others on their team.


How many ambassadors will there be?
Beyond only have one lead ambassador for each major life challenge we support (currently 15), there are no limits on how many ambassadors can be on our teams.
