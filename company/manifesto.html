Our shared values and beliefs: the foundation of The Intentional Living Collective community


Our core values (click on the value for our definition of it)

1. Community
We value all member voices and strive for productive and healthy, mutually beneficial collaborations for all.

2. Safety
We offer safe space for individuals and organizations to connect.

3. Integrity
We let our actions reflect our values and strive to meet all commitments. If a commitment can’t be met we will correct our actions in a timely and effective manner.

4. Respectful
We treat all members with respect at all times and use language that reflects appreciation of their wisdom and perspective.

5. Fair
We are inclusive, fair, and equitable in all our transactions and interactions with all members.

6. Supportive & inspiring
We use words and actions with all members that encourage and uplift them on their individual and collective journeys, inspiring them be their best, authentic selves. We lead by example to show positive ways for others.

7. Constructive
We address disagreements constructively, giving others the benefit of the doubt and attacking problems in collaborative ways rather than attacking the people.

8. Organic
We interact with all members in a way that allows things to unfold in the most natural way possible



What we believe:



1


That love is the foundation for a truly happy life.

Love is the energy that connects us all together, overcoming hatred and division. Love is the light that wins out over the darkness of fear.


2


That with hope and inspiration, all things are possible.

“Hope is being able to see that there is light despite all of the darkness.”– Desmond Tutu "There's no need to be perfect to inspire others. Let people get inspired by how you deal with your imperfections." - Kurt Tasche

We believe that hope can lead to inspiration, inspiration can lead to inspired action, inspired action can lead to personal evolution, and that enough personal evolution can lead to societal revolution.


3


That authentic community connections are vitally important to our happiness.

They lead to positive, meaningful interactions and can give people a true sense of belonging to something bigger than themselves.


4


That love and joy make life worthwhile.

Love and joy can be found through authentic community connections, and without it we can feel isolated and empty.


5


That there is still mystery in the world, and it is good.

Embracing the practical magic that can be found in authentic community allows that mystery to unfold in our lives.





To ensure a safe and supportive environment for all of our community members, we ask you to uphold these values and beliefs in your interactions with others in our community.
